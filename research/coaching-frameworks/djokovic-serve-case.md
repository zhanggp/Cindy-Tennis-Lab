## Role In Cindy Tennis Lab

Djokovic is a professional case study source. The case is useful because it
shows how an elite player can integrate serve, movement, return pressure,
physical capacity, and competitive decision-making.

It should inform the Coach Agent, Knowledge Graph Agent, Research Agent, Content
Agent, and Physical Performance Agent.

## Case Framing

This is not a model for direct imitation by a junior learner.

Professional cases should be used to ask better questions:

- What does efficient serve execution look like at a high level?
- Which serve metrics matter beyond raw speed?
- How does serve connect to the next ball and total point pressure?
- What movement and recovery qualities support the serve pattern?

## Extracted Observations

- ATP exposes Djokovic's official player profile and player stats pages.
- ATP's stats taxonomy separates serve, return, and pressure views.
- ATP serve-stat categories include service games won, total service points
  won, aces, double faults, first-serve percentage, first-serve points won,
  second-serve points won, break points saved, and break points faced.

## Claim Candidates

- Serve evaluation should include efficiency and point outcomes, not just ball
  speed.
- Professional serve cases should be mapped to metrics and principles before
  being translated into drills.
- Elite-player case studies are implementation examples, not standard answers.
