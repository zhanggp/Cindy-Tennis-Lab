# System Overview

Cindy Tennis Lab is organized as a learning system rather than a single app.
The repository is the source of truth for principles, structured knowledge,
experiments, and reflection.

## System Layers

### Foundation

The foundation layer defines the ideas that should remain stable:

- Understanding is more important than imitation.
- 理解，比模仿更重要。
- First Principles
- Evidence Driven
- Engineering Thinking
- Continuous Iteration
- Long-termism

### Knowledge

The knowledge layer stores structured tennis concepts and observations. It
should connect what happened, what was observed, what principle may explain it,
and what action should be tested next.

### Experiments

The experiment layer separates learner-specific work.

- Mickey: adult learner, 2.5 to 4.0.
- Cindy: junior learner, NCAA pathway.

Each learner can have different goals, constraints, and evidence while still
sharing the same principles.

### Journal

The journal layer creates continuity. Daily or periodic notes preserve what was
learned, what changed, what remains unclear, and what should be tried next.

## Feedback Loop

1. Observe practice or match evidence.
2. Describe the evidence in structured notes.
3. Connect the observation to a principle or hypothesis.
4. Choose a small next experiment.
5. Review the result.
6. Iterate.

## AI Integration

AI is a companion layer across the system. It can help summarize, structure,
compare, and question. It should not become the authority by default.

AI does not replace learning. AI amplifies learning.

AI does not replace parents. AI amplifies companionship.
