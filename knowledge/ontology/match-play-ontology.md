# Match Play Ontology

The match play ontology describes how technical skills become decisions under
pressure.

## Core Questions

- What pattern is the player trying to run?
- What does the opponent dislike?
- When should the player defend, neutralize, or attack?
- What breaks down under pressure?
- What should be trained next?

## Evidence

Use match notes, point patterns, serve plus one outcomes, return outcomes,
unforced error clusters, and emotional-state notes.

## Next Expansion

Define point construction, serve patterns, return patterns, pressure points,
scoreboard management, and post-match review.
