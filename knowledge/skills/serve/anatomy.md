# Serve Anatomy

Anatomy describes body structures and safety constraints that shape serve
development.

This file is not a medical guide and does not diagnose injuries.

## Key Areas

| Area | Serve Relevance |
| --- | --- |
| Shoulder | Overhead range, control, and deceleration |
| Scapula | Upward rotation and shoulder support |
| Trunk | Rotation, extension, and energy transfer |
| Hips | Loading, rotation, and balance |
| Knees | Loading and upward drive |
| Ankles and feet | Push-off, landing, and recovery |

## Cindy Guardrails

For a junior learner, serve development should account for:

- growth and coordination changes
- shoulder and elbow comfort
- foot or heel pain
- fatigue after high serve volume
- landing and recovery quality

## Inputs

- health notes
- pain reports
- rehab or coach feedback
- serve volume
- video of landing and recovery

## Outputs

- safety constraints
- stop conditions
- experiment guardrails
- questions for coach or qualified health professional
