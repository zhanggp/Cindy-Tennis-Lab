# First Principles

These first principles guide Cindy Tennis Lab.

## 1. Understanding Before Imitation

Understanding is more important than imitation.

理解，比模仿更重要。

Imitation can create movement. Understanding creates adaptability.

## 2. Evidence Before Opinion

Training decisions should be grounded in observable evidence whenever possible:
video, match notes, coach feedback, statistics, body feel, and repeated patterns.

Evidence does not remove judgment. It improves judgment.

## 3. Systems Before Isolated Fixes

A missed shot is rarely only one thing. It may involve preparation, spacing,
contact point, balance, decision making, recovery, pressure, or opponent
patterns.

Engineering thinking helps us ask: what system produced this result?

## 4. Small Iterations Before Big Claims

Progress should be tested through small, clear experiments.

One practice can test one hypothesis. One match can reveal one pattern. One
review can improve one next action.

## 5. Long-Termism Before Short-Term Anxiety

The goal is not to win every week at the cost of growth.

Mickey's path from 2.5 toward 4.0 and Cindy's possible NCAA path both require
patience, memory, and durable habits.

## 6. AI as Amplifier

AI does not replace learning. AI amplifies learning.

AI does not replace parents. AI amplifies companionship.

The human journey remains central. AI helps us see, remember, ask, and iterate.
